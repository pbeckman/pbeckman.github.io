BIO

I am currently a PhD student at Courant advised by Mike O'Neil and supported by the DOE CSGF. My research focuses on using tools from classical and modern numerical analysis to develop fast algorithms for statistical modeling of physical systems.

I received my undergraduate degree in Computational and Applied Mathematics at the University of Chicago, and worked as a math and statistics researcher at Argonne National Lab with Mihai Anitescu and Michael Stein. I have also had internships at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab with Chao Yang and at Lawrence Livermore National Lab with Daniel Osei-Kuffuor.

EQUITY IN MATH

I am strongly committed to working to make mathematics more accessible, supportive, and equitable for individuals who have been historically marginalized in the mathematical sciences. To this end, I am a tutor with the Petey Greene Program, which is a fantastic organization supporting the educational goals of currently and formerly incarcerated people from high school equivalency through college. I also regularly attend and help lead discussions in the Courant Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Reading Group.
